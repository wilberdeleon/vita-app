## Purpose

This document defines how VITA is built, how decisions are made, and the principles that guide the company. While features, technology, and the team may evolve, these principles should remain constant.

---

## 1. Build Something We'd Use Every Day

If we wouldn't personally use a feature, we shouldn't build it. Every screen should solve a real problem and every interaction should feel intentional.

## 2. Improve People's Lives

Our mission is not to maximize screen time—it is to improve health. Every feature should help users become healthier, more consistent, or more aware of their progress.

## 3. Simplicity Wins

The best solution is usually the simplest one. If a feature requires a long explanation, it likely needs to be redesigned.

## 4. Premium Over Flashy

VITA should feel calm, modern, and trustworthy. We don't chase trends, clutter the interface, or copy competitors. Every design decision should feel timeless.

## 5. Earn Trust

Health is personal. Trust is our most valuable asset. We never sacrifice user trust for short-term growth.

## 6. Progress Over Perfection

Users should never feel punished for missing a day. VITA encourages consistency, not perfection. Every day is a chance to continue the journey.

## 7. Build in Slices

Every feature must be developed in small, manageable slices. Each slice should have:

- A clear objective
- A defined scope
- A review
- An audit
- Final approval before completion

## 8. One Source of Truth

Every type of information has one official home.

- Product Bible → Product vision
- Build Handbook → Team workflow
- Design System → UI/UX standards
- Roadmap → Future priorities
- Slice Tracker → Development progress
- Audit Log → Findings and recommendations
- Changelog → Release history

Avoid duplicate documentation.

## 9. Debate Ideas, Never People

Great products come from healthy discussions. Challenge ideas, not individuals. Always assume good intent.

## 10. Leave It Better Than You Found It

Every contribution—whether code, design, documentation, or testing—should leave VITA cleaner, clearer, and stronger than before.

## 11. Quality Is a Feature

Performance, accessibility, polish, and attention to detail are features—not luxuries.

## 12. Think Long-Term

We're building a product people can rely on for years, not months. Every decision should support that vision.

## 13. Health Comes First

Growth, revenue, and new features never come before helping people improve their health. If a decision conflicts with our mission, the mission wins.

## 14. Build for the Next 10 Years

Before adding any feature, ask:

- Will this still make sense in five years?
- Does it simplify or complicate the product?
- Will new users immediately understand it?
- Does it strengthen VITA's mission?

If the answer is no, don't build it.

---

# Founder's Commitment

As founders, we commit to building VITA with integrity, curiosity, discipline, and long-term thinking.

We will continue learning.
We will continue improving.
We will listen to our users.
We will challenge our own assumptions.

Above all, we will never lose sight of why VITA exists:

**To help people become the healthiest version of themselves, one small decision at a time.**
