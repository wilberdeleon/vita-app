# Future Features

**What is this?** The strategic narrative for where Vita goes beyond Version 1 — why these directions matter, not the detailed proposals for each one. Full proposals now live in the [[Innovation Lab]]; this page is the "why this arc" story that connects them to the product's mission.

**Why does it exist?** These directions define where Vita is going (a longevity platform, not a tracker). Documenting the arc keeps V1 decisions compatible with it, without duplicating the idea-by-idea detail that belongs in the Lab.

*Source: founder direction (2026-07-06) plus the repo Master Roadmap's long-term list. **None of these exist in code.** Nothing here is committed until it enters the [[Roadmap]] via the VITA Promise test.*

Three ideas that form one arc: shifting Vita from "what you did today" to "how you are aging" — the deepest expression of *transformation over tracking* ([[Mission & Vision]]).

| Biomarker Age | The deeper, lab-grounded metric that follows it | [[Biomarker Age]] |

## Dependencies

All of it depends on live data (Sprints 1–5) and most of it on Atlas foundations ([[Future AI]]). The longevity trio needs scientific grounding before design — **the biggest open research question in the product.**

## Open questions

- What is defensible scientifically vs. what is marketing? Health claims intersect with trust (principle #5) and possibly regulation. *Owner: founders, before any longevity feature is designed.*
