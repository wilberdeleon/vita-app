# Core Principles

**What is this?** The Founder's Principles — how Vita is built, how decisions are made, and what stays constant while everything else evolves.

**Why does it exist?** Features, technology, and the team will change. These principles should not.

*Source: verified from the repo Founder's Principles (`docs/00-Founders-Principles.md`), v1.0, July 2026. Founders: **Wilber De Leon** and **Santiago Vazquez**.*

---

## The fourteen principles

1. **Build something we'd use every day.** If we wouldn't personally use a feature, we shouldn't build it.
2. **Improve people's lives.** The mission is health, not screen time.
3. **Simplicity wins.** If a feature needs a long explanation, it needs a redesign.
4. **Premium over flashy.** Calm, modern, trustworthy, timeless. No trend-chasing, no clutter, no copying competitors.
5. **Earn trust.** Health is personal; trust is the most valuable asset. Never sacrifice it for short-term growth.
6. **Progress over perfection.** Users are never punished for missing a day. Consistency, not perfection.
7. **Build in slices.** Every feature ships as a small slice with a clear objective, defined scope, review, audit, and founder approval.
8. **One source of truth.** Every type of information has exactly one official home. Never duplicate documentation.
9. **Debate ideas, never people.** Challenge ideas, assume good intent.
10. **Leave it better than you found it.** Every contribution makes Vita cleaner, clearer, stronger.
11. **Quality is a feature.** Performance, accessibility, and polish are not luxuries.
12. **Think long-term.** Build for years, not months.
13. **Health comes first.** Growth, revenue, and features never outrank helping people improve their health.
14. **Build for the next 10 years.** Before adding any feature ask: Will it still make sense in five years? Does it simplify or complicate? Will new users immediately understand it? Does it strengthen the mission? If no — don't build it.

## The founders' commitment

To build Vita with integrity, curiosity, discipline, and long-term thinking. To keep learning, keep improving, listen to users, and challenge our own assumptions — without ever losing sight of why Vita exists:

> **To help people become the healthiest version of themselves, one small decision at a time.**
