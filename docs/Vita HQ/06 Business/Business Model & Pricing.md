# Business Model & Pricing

**What is this?** How Vita will make money — the model, the constraints, and the open decisions.

**Why does it exist?** Pricing decisions shape product decisions (especially AI cost — [[Future AI]]). Documenting the constraints now prevents accidental lock-in later.

*Status: **no business model has been decided or documented anywhere** (verified: nothing in the repo; no founder statement on pricing). This page holds the constraints and questions, not answers.*

---

## What we know (constraints from the product's own principles)

- **Premium positioning.** The Apple/Oura/WHOOP quality bar implies premium pricing is credible — and expected. A premium transformation platform priced like a free calorie counter undercuts its own story.
- **Trust boundaries.** "Never sacrifice user trust for short-term growth" ([[Core Principles]] #5) effectively rules out selling health data and dark-pattern upsells. Health comes before revenue (#13) — pricing can't gate safety-relevant features punitively.
- **AI marginal cost.** Once Atlas coaches, each active user costs real tokens monthly ([[Future AI]]). Subscription (not one-time purchase) is the only model that survives that math.
- **Comparable market:** WHOOP and Oura are subscription businesses; premium health apps cluster around subscription with trial. See [[Competitive Landscape]].

## Tier vocabulary in use (not a pricing decision)

The [[Innovation Lab]]'s idea template tags every idea with a **Recommended Tier: Free / Pro / Atlas / Elite / Internal** for triage purposes (see [[Premium Opportunities]]). This names four candidate tiers but **does not constitute a tier or pricing decision** — no structure, pricing, or feature-gating has been decided. Treat these names as a working taxonomy the founders may keep, rename, or discard entirely when this page's open decisions are actually resolved.

**Signal worth a direct conversation — now three for three.** The founders have independently described three ideas — [[Mobile Order Screenshot Import]], [[AI Meal Photo Recognition]], and [[Smart Fridge Scanner]] — as "**Atlas Premium**" features, a phrase that doesn't match any existing tier name. All three were mapped to the plain **Atlas** tier for now, but the same specific, unprompted phrase appearing three times in a row is a strong pattern, not a coincidence. This is very likely a real tier name in the founders' heads (possibly "Atlas Premium" as a sub-tier above plain Atlas, gating vision-based AI capabilities specifically) rather than something to keep inferring idea by idea — worth resolving directly at the next founder pricing conversation.

**First mixed-tier idea:** [[Apple Home Screen Widgets]] is the Lab's first idea to explicitly split tiers *within itself* — most widgets Free, Atlas-powered widgets gated to Atlas tier — rather than proposing one tier for the whole feature. Useful precedent: it suggests the real tier boundary the founders have in mind may be "static data display" (Free) vs. "ongoing AI computation" (paid), which lines up with why Atlas-tier ideas elsewhere in the Lab all involve live generation or coaching, not just data lookup.

## Open decisions (founders)

1. Subscription price point and tiers (free tier? trial-only? Atlas as the premium tier?)
2. When pricing enters the product — V1 launch, or free during early access?
3. Whether hardware-free positioning ("WHOOP insight without the strap") becomes a pricing argument
4. App Store commission planning (15% small-business rate likely applies initially)

## Recommendation to resolve this page

A founder pricing discussion once **Sprint 6 — Premium** is underway (when the product's premium feel is real — see [[Roadmap]], which also includes a Subscription Experience slice there), documented here and in the [[Decision Log]]. Pricing before polish would price the wrong product.
